LearnWhat is Ramadan?

Use the age buttons to keep the page simple for younger children or richer for older children.

🌙

Ramadan is a blessed month

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar. Muslim families try to worship Allah more and become kinder.

  • Say: Ramadan is special.
  • Do: Smile and help someone today.
📖

The Qur’an was revealed in Ramadan

Ramadan reminds Muslims to love the Qur’an, listen carefully, recite gently, and follow Allah’s guidance.

  • Look: Find your Qur’an shelf.
  • Say: I love Allah’s Book.
🍽️

Fasting teaches self-control

Capable adult Muslims fast from dawn to sunset. Young children are not required to fast, but they can practice patience and gratitude.

  • Practice: Wait politely for your turn.
  • Remember: Allah loves sincere effort.
🤲

Dua is a beautiful habit

In Ramadan, families make more dua. Children can ask Allah for a clean heart, good manners, and love for parents.

  • Say: O Allah, make me kind.
  • Do: Raise your hands gently.
🕌

Prayer becomes more special

Muslims pray five times every day. In Ramadan, many families also go to the masjid and listen to Qur’an at night.

  • Child task: Prepare your prayer mat.
  • Connect: Learn more on the Prayer page.
🥣

Suhoor and iftar

Suhoor is the meal before dawn. Iftar is the meal at sunset. Children can help set dates, water, plates, and napkins.

  • Say: Bismillah before eating.
  • Say: Alhamdulillah after eating.
🌌

Laylat al-Qadr

Laylat al-Qadr is a very blessed night in Ramadan. Muslims pray, recite Qur’an, and ask Allah for forgiveness and goodness.

  • Do: Make a quiet dua before sleep.
  • Learn: Surah Al-Qadr with a teacher.
💝

Charity and sharing

Ramadan teaches us to care for hungry, lonely, poor, and sad people. Even a child can share a toy, snack, or kind word.

  • Do: Put one coin in a charity box.
  • Say: Sharing makes hearts happy.
🧠

What fasting is not

Ramadan is not only about staying hungry. It is about obeying Allah, controlling anger, speaking truth, and improving manners.

  • Stop: Shouting and grabbing.
  • Start: Saying please and sorry.

RoutineA simple Ramadan day

This is a gentle family routine. Parents may adjust it according to age, health, school time, and local prayer times.

1
Before dawn: SuhoorEat with family. Say Bismillah. Thank Allah for food.
2
Morning: Good mannersSpeak softly. Help parents. Keep toys and books tidy.
3
Afternoon: Qur’an timeListen to one short surah or revise a short dua.
4
Sunset: IftarHelp set the table. Wait calmly. Say Alhamdulillah.
5
Night: Family worshipPray, make dua, listen, and sleep with a clean heart.
🧒

Important note for children

Small children are loved by Allah. They do not need to copy adult fasting. They can join Ramadan through love, manners, duas, Qur’an listening, and small good deeds.

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً

Meaning: Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter.

CelebrateWhat is Eid?

Eid is halal happiness: worship Allah, thank Him, meet family, share food, give gifts, and keep beautiful manners.

🎉

Eid al-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr comes after Ramadan. Muslims thank Allah for helping them worship, fast, give charity, pray, and improve their manners.

  • Wear clean and nice clothes.
  • Say Eid Mubarak with a smile.
  • Share food and happiness.
  • Remember poor and needy people.
🐑

Eid al-Adha

Eid al-Adha is connected with Hajj and the story of Prophet Ibrahim عليه السلام. It teaches obedience, sacrifice, sharing, and trust in Allah.

  • Thank Allah for blessings.
  • Listen to the story with parents.
  • Share meat and food with others.
  • Be gentle to animals and people.

MannersEid day checklist

A clear visual checklist for children before leaving home or meeting guests.

🛁

Clean body

Take a bath with parent help, brush teeth, comb hair, and get ready with cleanliness.

👕

Nice clothes

Wear clean clothes. Expensive clothes are not required; neatness and gratitude matter.

🕌

Eid prayer

Go with family when possible. Listen quietly, follow elders, and keep the masjid clean.

🤝

Greetings

Say “Eid Mubarak” with a smile. Hug or shake hands only as your family teaches.

🍲

Share food

Eat with Bismillah, thank Allah, and help serve guests kindly.

🧸

Share joy

Play nicely, take turns, avoid fighting, and include younger children.

Parents & TeachersHow to teach this page

Keep Ramadan learning warm, age-appropriate, visual, and pressure-free.

For ages 3–4

  • Use pictures, names, and actions.
  • Do not overload with rules.
  • Teach: Bismillah, Alhamdulillah, smile, share.
  • Keep every task under two minutes.

For ages 5–6

  • Add short meanings: fasting teaches patience.
  • Let the child help with iftar table tasks.
  • Use good deeds jar daily.
  • Repeat one dua every few days.

For ages 7–8

  • Explain Ramadan as worship, manners, and self-control.
  • Introduce Laylat al-Qadr gently.
  • Let them write one Ramadan reflection.
  • Discuss charity and caring for others.
Content foundation: This page uses simple, child-ready explanations based on well-known Islamic teachings: fasting in Ramadan, Qur’an guidance, dua, prayer, charity, good manners, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, and the educational principle that young children learn best through short, concrete, visual, repeated activities. Parents should consult a qualified local scholar for detailed legal rulings.
