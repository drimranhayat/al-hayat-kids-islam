🧭 Simple daily routine

10–15 minutes

Start small

Open one level or topic. Choose 3 to 5 cards only. Young children learn better in short sessions.

Read together

Read the question aloud. Let the child answer. Praise effort before correcting the answer.

Practise gently

Use the activity, practice and game prompts. Keep the tone warm and playful.

Give a star

Mark cards complete and open badges. Celebrate progress without pressure or comparison.

✅ How parents should use each section

Best order
  • Questions: Use this as the main learning bank. Filter by level, topic, type or difficulty.
  • Activities: Use after questions. Children tap, say, choose, repeat and complete small tasks.
  • Games: Use for revision. Keep the mood light and avoid making it a stressful test.
  • Practice: Use the typing board and whiteboard for Arabic words, duas, Islamic terms and short answers.
  • Progress: Check completed cards and stars on the same device/browser the child used.
  • Badges: Let the child see unlocked badges as encouragement, not as competition with others.

🛡️ Safety, privacy and adult supervision

Important
Privacy note: Progress is saved on the child’s own device using browser storage. The site should avoid collecting unnecessary personal information from children. Parents should supervise children when using any website.
Content note: This website is designed for gentle early Islamic learning. Parents and teachers should review the content before formal classroom use, especially Arabic pronunciation, local school policy and age suitability.

💬 Parent FAQs

Quick answers

How many questions should a child do daily?

For ages 3–5, 3 to 5 cards are enough. For ages 6–8, 5 to 10 cards can work if the child remains interested.

Should children memorize everything?

No. At this age, love, recognition, repetition and positive habits matter more than pressure-based memorization.

Can teachers use it in class?

Yes. Teachers can use level filters, topic filters and practice pages for short warm-ups, revision and classroom activities.

Why are stars saved only on this device?

The current system uses local browser storage, so progress appears on the same device and browser used by the child.

Al-Hayat Kids Islam is meant to support parents and teachers in building love for Islamic learning through simple, colourful and age-appropriate practice. Keep every session kind, short and joyful.
