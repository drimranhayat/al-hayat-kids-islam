Islamic Manners for Children

Akhlaq becomes meaningful when children practice manners in real home, school, and play situations.

Real-life akhlaq practice

Scenario learning children can understand

Manners become powerful when the child sees what to do in normal home and school situations.

👋

Meeting someone

What should we say? Assalamu Alaikum, with a smile and kind voice.

🍽️

At mealtime

Say Bismillah, eat calmly, thank Allah, and do not waste food.

🧸

With friends

Share, wait for your turn, say sorry, and forgive.

Akhlaq as daily Islamic practice

Young children understand manners through actions: saying Salam, sharing toys, helping parents, speaking truth, and using kind words.

Scenario-based learning examples

Ask simple questions: What should we say when we meet someone? What should we do if we hurt a friend? How do we treat animals kindly?

Gentle correction

Parents should model the manner first, correct calmly, and praise effort. Manners grow through repetition and family example.
