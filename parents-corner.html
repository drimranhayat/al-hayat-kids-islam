Parents Corner

How parents and teachers should use the app

The app is designed to support adults, not replace them. Use short, loving, real-life routines and guide pronunciation gently.

5-minute daily Islamic routine

Choose one small topic, read one card, repeat one phrase, ask one easy question, and practice one real-life action such as saying Bismillah before eating.

How to teach duas

Teach duas at the moment they are used. Keep it natural: before food, before sleep, after waking, before study, and when thanking someone.

How to help memorization

Use short repetition, visual cues, praise, and calm correction. Never shame a child for forgetting.

How to explain Allah lovingly

Use simple, warm language about Allah’s mercy, creation, care, blessings, and love for good actions. Avoid fear-heavy explanations for young children.

Safe screen-time guidance

Use the app with a parent beside the child. Keep sessions short, stop before tiredness, and connect screen learning to real family routines.

Weekly learning plan

Ages 3–4

Three days of Islamic words, two days of manners, one day of family gratitude, one day of review through pictures.

Ages 5–6

Two duas, one Arabic letter group, one short Surah review, one Prophet story, one manners scenario, one good deed day.

Ages 7–8

Dua with meaning, Salah names, wudu steps, short Surah revision, Seerah timeline, Prophets’ story lesson, and daily akhlaq challenge.
