Curriculum overview

Complete Islamic learning map for ages 3–8

A parent-guided, age-wise and level-wise curriculum covering belief, duas, Qur’an love, Surahs, Prophets, manners, prayer basics, Ramadan, Arabic letters, and daily Islamic habits.

Curriculum architecture

Built like a complete early Islamic learning ecosystem

Every topic is connected to an age path, a parent role, an activity style, and a daily-life outcome.

🌱

Age-wise

Simple recognition for 3–4, repetition and matching for 5–6, understanding and responsibility for 7–8.

🧭

Level-wise

Look and Learn, Practice and Remember, Use in Daily Life.

🏡

Home-linked

Every lesson points toward a real family routine, not just screen time.

Allah and Iman

What children learn
Children learn that Allah created and cares for them, using gentle, loving, age-appropriate language.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Read the short prompt and connect it to family, nature, and gratitude.
Learning method
Look, talk, repeat, and notice blessings.
App activity example
Child taps pictures of family, sky, animals, and says “Allah made this.”

Kalimah and Basic Belief

What children learn
Simple exposure to the First Kalimah and basic Muslim identity without pressure.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Say the words slowly and explain only the simple meaning suitable for the child.
Learning method
Repeat with parent, match key words, and review gently.
App activity example
A card asks: “Who do Muslims worship?” with simple answer choices.

Daily Duas

What children learn
Routine duas for eating, sleeping, waking, entering/leaving home, knowledge, and parents.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Model pronunciation and help the child use duas in real moments.
Learning method
Routine-based memorization and picture prompts.
App activity example
Before-eating card: child chooses the meal picture and parent reads the dua.

Short Surahs

What children learn
Respectful introduction to short Surahs with parent or teacher pronunciation support.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Listen to the child, correct gently, and consult a qualified reciter/teacher for accuracy.
Learning method
Small memorization goals and revision checklists.
App activity example
Surah progress card with “heard, repeated, remembered” badges.

Qur’an Love and Etiquette

What children learn
Children learn to respect the Qur’an, keep clean hands, listen quietly, and handle it with care.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Show real-life adab and avoid turning etiquette into fear.
Learning method
Visual scenarios and yes/no choices.
App activity example
Child chooses the respectful action before touching a mushaf.

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

What children learn
Love, kindness, truthfulness, mercy, and basic Seerah moments in simple language.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Share short, verified, gentle stories and connect them to daily manners.
Learning method
Story cards, moral reflection, and action prompts.
App activity example
“Choose the kind action” after a child-friendly Seerah moment.

Prophets’ Stories

What children learn
Age-appropriate stories of selected Prophets with moral lessons and no fabricated details.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Keep stories short, positive, and authentic; avoid unsupported details.
Learning method
Picture sequence, moral lesson, and discussion.
App activity example
Arrange three picture cards showing beginning, challenge, and lesson.

Islamic Manners / Akhlaq

What children learn
Salam, truthfulness, respect, kindness, sharing, cleanliness, and forgiveness.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Praise small actions when the child uses manners in daily life.
Learning method
Scenario-based learning and habit practice.
App activity example
Child selects what to say when meeting someone: “Assalamu Alaikum.”

Cleanliness and Wudu

What children learn
Clean hands, clean clothes, hygiene, and awareness that Muslims prepare for prayer.
Age suitability
4–8
Parent role
Demonstrate cleanliness and wudu gently; no complex fiqh debates.
Learning method
Routine visuals and checklist cards.
App activity example
A “clean before prayer” checklist with hands, face, clothes, place.

Salah Basics

What children learn
Names of five prayers, prayer place, Qiblah awareness, and simple prayer habits.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Let the child watch, imitate, and build respect for Salah slowly.
Learning method
Visual schedule, naming, and gentle practice.
App activity example
Match Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha with time-of-day pictures.

Ramadan and Fasting

What children learn
Ramadan love, suhoor/iftar awareness, Qur’an, charity, kindness, and Eid.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Invite children into Ramadan routines without pressure to fast before readiness.
Learning method
Calendar stickers, good deeds, family routine.
App activity example
Child adds a star for helping at iftar or sharing food.

Eid and Islamic Months

What children learn
Basic awareness of Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Dhul Hijjah, and Islamic calendar themes.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Explain celebrations with gratitude, family, worship, and kindness.
Learning method
Story prompts and celebration cards.
App activity example
Choose kind Eid actions: greet family, share, thank Allah.

Masjid Manners

What children learn
Quiet voices, clean clothes, walking calmly, respecting worshippers, and staying with parents.
Age suitability
4–8
Parent role
Prepare the child before visiting the masjid and guide calmly.
Learning method
Before/after visit checklist.
App activity example
Tap the correct masjid behavior: walk gently, listen, stay nearby.

Arabic Letters

What children learn
Recognition of Arabic letters from Alif to Yaa, early shapes, and Islamic words.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Point, trace, repeat letter names, and connect letters to simple words.
Learning method
Tracing-style cards and matching.
App activity example
Trace Alif, identify Baa, match letter shapes.

Islamic Words

What children learn
Simple words such as Allah, Islam, Muslim, Qur’an, Salah, Dua, Salam, and Masjid.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Use words naturally in home routines and explain them simply.
Learning method
Word-picture matching and repetition.
App activity example
Match “Masjid” to a mosque icon.

Family and Parents

What children learn
Respect, gratitude, helping at home, dua for parents, and kind family speech.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Turn family moments into learning moments without lectures.
Learning method
Real-life habits and role-play.
App activity example
Choose what to say when parent helps: “JazakAllahu khayran.”

Kindness and Good Deeds

What children learn
Helping, sharing, feeding, smiling, forgiving, and caring for animals and nature.
Age suitability
3–8
Parent role
Notice and praise effort, not perfection.
Learning method
Good deed challenges and stars.
App activity example
Complete one kind action today and mark it with a star.

Halal Daily Habits

What children learn
Eating with Bismillah, gratitude, cleanliness, modest behavior, and avoiding waste.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Guide with routine reminders and examples.
Learning method
Daily checklist and scenario choices.
App activity example
Choose the better habit: drink water calmly and do not waste.

Islamic Quiz

What children learn
Gentle review quizzes for recognition, memory, and daily-life understanding.
Age suitability
5–8
Parent role
Treat quizzes as play, not exams.
Learning method
Tap answer, match, sort, and review.
App activity example
“Which phrase do we say before eating?” with picture clues.

Parents Corner

What children learn
Guidance for parents and teachers using the app in short, loving daily routines.
Age suitability
Adults
Parent role
Use the website/app as a companion, not a replacement for parenting or teaching.
Learning method
5-minute routines, plans, and correction requests.
App activity example
Parent chooses a weekly plan for the child’s age group.

Level-wise learning system

The same curriculum becomes easier or deeper according to the child’s age and readiness.

Level 1

Look and Learn

Picture-based learning, very short text, parent reads, child observes and repeats.

Level 2

Practice and Remember

Matching, tap the correct answer, checklists, repeat with parent, and memory tasks.

Level 3

Use in Daily Life

Scenario questions, good deed challenges, daily habit tracker, and star/badge completion.
