Learning modes

Lessons, activities, and mini games

📘

Lesson

One clear idea, one picture-like symbol, very short words, and parent support only when needed.

🧩

Activity

Match, tap, trace, sort, checklist, repeat, and practice through real family routines.

🎮

Mini Game

Simple no-audio games: choose the right action, collect a star, complete a kindness challenge.

Parent and teacher map

Guidance moved lower, where adults can read it

The child-facing page should not start with long explanations. Adult notes are still here for trust, SEO, and classroom/home planning.

Age-wise design

Age 3–4 uses recognition and simple Islamic words. Age 5–6 uses repetition, matching, and short memorization. Age 7–8 uses understanding, scenarios, responsibility, and daily practice.

Level-wise design

Level 1 is Look and Learn. Level 2 is Practice and Remember. Level 3 is Use in Daily Life. The same topic becomes easier or deeper depending on readiness.

Authenticity rule

No fabricated Qur’an, Hadith, duas, virtues, rewards, or story details. Exact Arabic text, transliteration, and translation need scholarly verification before publication.

Safety rule

No ads, no login, no tracking, no child chat, no microphone, no camera, no unsafe external links, and no audio in the first version.
